Shantou

About Shantou

Shantou is one of the cities in Guangdong, China, which is where my parents from. This is a place with full of traditional cultures where people here maintain most part of religious rituals adapting from ancient China. Seafood might be one of the most identical cuisines in Shantou. As a coastal city in South China, people around here consider seafood as their dominant part of the diet. Fish, crab, shrimp, and scallop around here are so fresh that some of them can even be eaten raw. What’s more, the hotpot serve with fresh and juicy beef is another signature in Shantou, which creates a distinctive hotpot style from Sichuan spicy hotpot.

Tips

  • Prepare for the hot weather and damp climate.
  • Argue with vendor if you think you are overpaid when you visit Shantou by yourself.
  • Trust the pink beef in hotpot.
  • It’s ok that not everyone can accept raw seafood.
  • Find a local tour guide is recommended because people in Shantou speak a dialect that sounds totally different from Mandarin.
  • There are no specific places to visit; all you need to do in Shantou is "go to a restaurant", "try their cuisines", and "find another restaurant".

Attractions

1. Chao Wei Ju Restaurant

Explore more

The one you can have the closest taste of Shantou cuisine in the US (but sill awful). It offers many traditional Shantou cuisines like beef hotpot and rice noodle, at least they’ve tried their best to introduce the food overseas. Their rice noodle is better than I expected.

2. Wu Ji Beef Soup

Explore more

“Wu Ji” stands for “Wu’s”, it a common way for Chinese people to name their shops by their last name. Serves the best beef hotpot and other beef-related cuisine. You can observe how the chef cuts and delivers the raw beef to your table. The slices of beef are so thin and fresh that is can be well-done to eat once you drop them into the pot for 8 seconds.

3. Zhao Ji Rice Noodle

Explore more

Same case, this restaurant is opened by a Zhao guy. I tried its pork rice noodle and it’s tasty! They will fry the chopped green onion and place into a bowl of lard and it becomes a topping for rice noodle with freshness.

4. Long Hu Downtown District

Explore more

“Long Hu” means dragon lake. It is one of the most well-developed district in Shantou with tons of restaurant. They are no “wrong choices”, only unique experience on Shantou cuisines.

5. Nan’ao Island

Explore more

It a island that need around 30-minute drive from Long Hu District. It is a small island with beautiful coastline highway and decent seafood.
